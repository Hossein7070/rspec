The standard, predefined macros, such as <code>FILE</code> and <code>LINE</code>, are primarily intended for use by the implementation, and changing them could result in undefined behavior.

This rule checks that the following predefined macros are not defined, undefined, or redefined: <code>assert</code>, <code>errno</code>, <code>FILE</code>, <code>LINE</code>, <code>TIME</code>, <code>DATE</code>, <code>TIMESTAMP</code>, <code>COUNTER</code>, <code>INCLUDE_LEVEL</code>, <code>BASE_FILE</code>, and <code>_Pragma</code>.

Noncompliant Code Example

#undef __LINE__

See

  • MISRA C:2004, 20.1 - Reserved identifiers, macros and functions in the standard library shall not be defined, redefined, or undefined
  • MISRA C++:2008, 17-0-1 - Reserved identifiers, macros and functions in the standard library shall not be defined, redefined, or undefined
  • MISRA C:2012, 21.1 - #define and #undef shall not be used on a reserved identifier or reserved macro name
